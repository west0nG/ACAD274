Analysis Examples

Here are two in-depth examples showing how to use this data to analyze team performance:

Example 1: LA Clippers Performance Analysis

The Clippers finished with a solid 51-31 record, averaging 115.6 points per game with strong shooting efficiency at 48.9% from the field and 38.1% from three-point range.

Their +3.3 point differential shows they were competitive throughout the season, though their 13.1 turnovers per game suggests room for improvement in ball control during crucial moments.

Example 2: Dallas Mavericks Playoff Run Analysis

Dallas built their 50-32 record on explosive offense, averaging 117.9 points per game with league-leading three-point production at 14.6 makes per game on 39.5 attempts.

Their modern offensive approach centered around Luka Dončić's playmaking, combined with strong offensive rebounding at 9.7 per game, provided the foundation for their surprising Finals run.

Key Terms

Understanding basketball statistics is crucial for analyzing team performance. Here are some important terms:

Three-Pointer
A shot taken from beyond the three-point line, worth 3 points when made
Turnover
An unforced error when a team loses possession of the ball
Steal
Taking the ball away from an opposing player
Block
Preventing an opponent's shot from going in by deflecting it
Free Throw
An unimpeded shot from 15 feet from the basket, worth 1 point
Rebound
Successfully retrieving the ball after a missed shot
Assist
A pass that directly leads to a teammate scoring
Plus/Minus
Team's point differential - points scored minus points allowed
Field Goal Percentage
Percentage of shots made from the field during the game
Personal Foul
A violation of the rules resulting in a penalty
